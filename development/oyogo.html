Oyogo!

{ Processing / online 8-bit drawing tool }

A ramen fantasy themed survival arcade-game created for my Programming Basics course. Inspired by my own ramen cooking obsession.

Oyogu 泳ぐ​​​​​​​ - (v.) to swim

The very nature of this concept was just calling for some mang-style aesthetics, so I decided to frame the design around this 8-bit pachinko look. I created the splash screen as well as all other assets of the game with an 8-bit drawing tool I found online.

Kajiru 齧る - (v.) to gnaw, nibble

After clicking on the ? button on the splash screen, the user would then be directed to the instruction screens. These screens were animated with pressing keys and moving game pieces to effectively demonstrate their purposes.

The main objective of this game is to collect enough "scallion coins" to fill the score bar, while trying to avoid as much damage caused by other floating food pieces as possible.

The game's difficulty increases as time progresses because Kaji, the main character, swells as he continues to eat his way through this fantasy. Collisions with other floating food pieces would result in some depletion of your stock (as represented by the hearts). The one exception to this would be colliding with a "magic corn" piece, which would then restore Kaji to his original size.
