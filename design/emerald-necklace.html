Emerald Necklace Experience & Systems Map

{ Adobe Illustrator / Adobe InDesign }

An experience map & systems map of Boston's Emerald Necklace created for my Design, Process, Context & Systems course.

I began this assignment by visiting Fenway Park for a few hours to simply observe its users. I took notes of everything and anything I noticed to give myself a wide scope to later select a focus from for my experience map. I noted details of the physical descriptions, interactions, as well as hypothesized intents of users. Occasionally, if a certain user's interaction with the park was particularly interesting, I would approach and spark conversation with them to gain some deeper insight.

I chose to focus my own mother as the user for this Emerald Necklace experience because being already so familiar with her background and personality, I knew I could effectively make conclusions about her responses. Also, my mother's tendency to be dramatic allowed for quite an entertaining range of responses.

For the beginning stages of designing the Emerald Necklace systems map, the class was grouped up according to experiences in order to most effectively process the great amount of information we needed to work with. Our final maps would be created individually. My group began tackling this by defining the separate parts of the Emerald Necklace (Boston Commons, Back Bay Fens, etc.). We then proceeded to define various systems (History, Plant Life, etc) and grouped specific examples to their corresponding parks. This rough, initial exercise ended up playing a significant role in hinting at how our final maps should be laid out.
